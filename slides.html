AI

Understanding the tech changing our world

What do you guys think, What is AI?

When they say AI, they almost always are actually talking about "Machine Learning"

  • What is Machine Learning?
  • think of those ReCaptcha challenges that ask "which images are a stop sign?"
  • you are training the computer to recognize stop signs.
  • Machine Learning: We feed the computer billions of examples (books, internet).
  • Neural Networks: Modeled loosely after the human brain (connections get stronger with practice).
  • It doesn't "know" facts; it predicts patterns.

And the vast majority of the time, We're talking about a specific thing called an "LLM"

  • Large Language Model
  • At it's core, it is a giant math equation that can predict the next word in a sentence.
  • Essentially, it "trains" itself by building a giant web of words and how they connect to each other. then it takes your input and uses that web to predict the next word.

What can you do with an LLM?

GIANT SUPER IMPORTANT RULE OF WHEN NOT TO USE AI

  • When you are in school, your teachers give you assignments and have you take tests to show what YOU know. If you use AI, you're cheating yourself. because that's not what YOU know, it's what IT knows.
  • So when the goal is "do x" or "get y done", use the heck out of AI.
  • But when it is "prove you understand z", stay way away from AI!

So, Back to what you can use it for...

  • Coding Assistant: It can write boilerplate code (Python, JS) instantly.
  • Creative Partner: Brainstorming D&D campaigns, YouTube scripts, or rap lyrics.
  • Personal Tutor: "Explain Quantum Physics to me like I'm 5."

Superhuman powers

  • It seems a little dramatic to say, and we are not there yet, but...
  • I really do see there being people that are like 10x productive because they are leveraging AI

The Future of Work

"AI won't replace you. A person using AI will replace you."

New skills needed:

  • Prompt Engineering: Knowing how to talk to the machine.
  • Context is king: One of the tricks with AI is to make sure you feed it with everything it needs to know to accomplish a task.
  • Critical Thinking: Knowing when the machine is wrong.

The "Hallucination" Trap

  • Warning: AI is a confident liar.
  • It predicts the next likely word, not the truth.
  • Example: Ask it for a biography of a fake person, and it might invent one.
  • Rule #1: Always fact-check.

The Dark Side (Be Aware)

  • Deepfakes: Voice cloning and face swapping. (Don't believe everything you see online).
  • Algorithmic Bias: If the training data is biased, the AI will be biased.
  • Data Privacy: Anything you type into a public AI might be used to train it. If it's free, YOU ARE THE PRODUCT!
